Heuristic functions and search guidance:

- use distance to nearest food as a heuristic value
- use distance to nearest ghost to penalize risk
- combine food and ghost heuristics with weights
- lower heuristic cost means a better move
- adjust heuristic weights to prefer safety or speed
- change condition thresholds based on ghost distance

Search algorithms and heuristics:
- DFS: deep exploration, may miss safe food
- BFS: finds shortest path, useful for early food
- A*: uses a heuristic plus path cost to find efficient routes
- heuristics should guide toward food while avoiding ghosts
- common heuristics: Manhattan distance, Manhattan(food, current), ghost distance

Heuristic examples:
- score_value = food_distance * 1.0 - ghost_distance * 2.0
- if ghost_distance < 3 then avoid ghosts first
- if food_distance is low then eat food now
